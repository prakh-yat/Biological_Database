Sarcoma

Sarcoma is a type of cancer that can occur in various locations in your body. Sarcoma is the general term for a broad group of cancers that begin in the bones and in the soft (also called connective) tissues (soft tissue sarcoma). Soft tissue sarcoma forms in the tissues that connect, support and surround other body structures. This includes muscle, fat, blood vessels, nerves, tendons and the lining of your joints.There are more than 70 types of sarcoma. Treatment for sarcoma varies depending on sarcoma type, location and other factors. Types of sarcoma:

  1. -Angiosarcoma
  2. -Chondrosarcoma
  3. -Ewing sarcoma
  4. -Liposarcoma
  5. -Osteosarcoma

Symptoms of sarcoma include:

  • -A lump that can be felt through the skin that may or may not be painful
  • -Bone pain
  • -A broken bone that happens unexpectedly, such as with a minor injury or no injury at all
  • -Ductal carcinoma in situ (DCIS)
  • -Abdominal pain
  • -Weight loss
